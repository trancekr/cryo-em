Section 1: Cryo-EM Overview
Electron Microscopy & Biological Samples
Why do we need cryo-EM?

⚠ The Problem

  • Vacuum damage — Water evaporates instantly in EM column vacuum
  • Radiation damage — High-energy electrons break chemical bonds
  • Fixation artifacts — Chemical fixation alters native conformation
  • Low contrast — Light atoms scatter electrons weakly
  • Charging — Non-conductive specimens accumulate charge
➔

✔ The Cryo Solution

  • Vitreous ice — Rapid cooling (~10⁴ C/s) in liquid ethane
  • Native state — Proteins remain in solution-like conformation
  • Reduced damage — Imaging at -196°C slows radiation damage
  • Low-dose imaging — 20-60 e⁻/Å²; compensated by averaging
  • No staining — Flash-freeze preserves native hydrated state
Dubochet et al., Q Rev Biophys (1988) · Glaeser, J Ultrastruct Res (1971) · Taylor & Glaeser, Science (1974)
