High frequencies (small features, high resolution) are towards the edges of the spectrum

Particle selection and extraction

Topaz – No general model; requires training per sample/per dataset. Is free standing or incorporated into RELION and cryoSPARC.

Warp – Has a general model but typically requires user re-training with specific sample types. Once trained, the neural network can work across different datasets and similar sample types. (Note: Warp only runs on Windows. Warp also has a junk detector that will automatically avoid junk based on a pre-trained neural network)

crYOLO – Has a general model that works well on most samples. Currently, is free standing or a part of the SPHIRE package.

Resolution – Bragg's Law

Resolution is calculated using Bragg’s law, which states that the smallest distance between crystal lattice planes that can be resolved (d) is equal to the wavelength of the X-ray (l) devided by twice the sine of the diffraction angle (q)

Resolution

Resolution is determined by calculating the Fourier Shell Correlation (FSC) between two independently generated ‘half-maps’ of the same data, signifying the point at which the correlation between the maps drops below a set threshold (commonly 0.143) across different resolution shells
