Section 2: Sample Preparation
The Circle of Cryo-EM Sample Preparation
Four key stages of sample preparation for cryo-EM
Sample Optimization
Maintain native state: pH, salt, detergents, cross-linking. Screen by negative stain before cryo-EM.
Sample Carriers & Treatments
3mm grid (Cu/Au mesh + holey foil). Plasma clean for wettability. Continuous film for particle distribution.
Deposition
Form 10–100 nm thin film on grid. Air-water interface causes denaturation — minimize deposition-to-freezing time.
Vitrification
Flash-freeze in liquid ethane (>10⁵ K/s) to form vitreous ice. Store below −137°C to prevent devitrification.
Weissenberger et al., Nat. Methods (2021)

Grid Preparation and Screening Time
Step 1 : Make 3× grids for every condition (reproducibility issue) → 3× sample & 3× time in the lab
Step 2 : Need to screen all the grids → 3× screening time + data collection
Step 3 : The samples may have issues → back to step 1 & 2 for repeating (multiple times)

Continuous Support Films
