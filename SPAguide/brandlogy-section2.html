Section 2: Sample Preparation
The Circle of Cryo-EM Sample Preparation
Four key stages of sample preparation for cryo-EM
Sample Optimization
Maintain native state: pH, salt, detergents, cross-linking. Screen by negative stain before cryo-EM.
Sample Carriers & Treatments
3mm grid (Cu/Au mesh + holey foil). Plasma clean for wettability. Continuous film for particle distribution.
Deposition
Form 10–100 nm thin film on grid. Air-water interface causes denaturation — minimize deposition-to-freezing time.
Vitrification
Flash-freeze in liquid ethane (>10⁵ K/s) to form vitreous ice. Store below −137°C to prevent devitrification.
Weissenberger et al., Nat. Methods (2021)

Homogeneity Analysis
Representative biophysical techniques to verify protein monodispersity before vitrification
SEC
Size Exclusion Chromatography
Separates particles by size — a single symmetric peak indicates monodispersity
SEC-MALS
+ Multi-Angle Light Scattering
Couples SEC with light scattering to give absolute molecular weight
DLS
Dynamic Light Scattering
Hydrodynamic radius from intensity fluctuations — narrow distribution = monodisperse
Mass Photometry
Single-molecule iSCAT
Single-molecule mass from interferometric scattering — resolves oligomeric states at picomolar

Grid Preparation and Screening Time
Multiple iterations are the practical bottleneck
The screening loop
Step 1 — Make 3× grids per condition (reproducibility) → 3× sample & 3× lab time
Step 2 — Screen every grid → 3× screening time + data collection
Step 3 — If samples have issues → back to Step 1 & 2, repeat multiple times
Initial sweep
1 sample × 2 conc. × 2 grid types × 3
= 12 grids
Refinement
1 conc. × 4 grid types × 3 → 12
or 1 conc. × 1 grid × 4 additives × 3 → 12
Final
1 conc. × 1 grid × 1 additive
= 1 grid

Section 2: Sample Preparation
Spot-to-Plunge Time Effect (with Chameleon)
Nanosecond-scale denaturation at the air-water interface
  • Particles can move 100 nm within 1 ms or less
  • Once adsorbed at the AWI, denaturation may follow in as little as a few nanoseconds
  • Sub-100 ms spot-to-plunge times are needed to outrun this damage
Noble et al., Nat. Methods (2018)

Continuous Support Films
Reduce AWI effects by keeping particles off the air-water interface
  • Single-Layer Graphene — Lowest background, best for small proteins
  • Graphene Oxide (GO) — Easier to prepare, reduces AWI, adds slight background
  • Amorphous Carbon — Most accessible, higher background, proven workflow
